Our Six-Month Journey

We followed a rigorous, user-centered design process to transform an idea into a tangible, trustworthy, and valuable solution for millions of CR members.

Phase 1:
Research & Discovery

We began with a broad challenge from the CR Innovation Lab: find the best problem to solve using agentic AI within CR’s ecosystem. This led us to conduct 27 consumer interviews to uncover the core needs of modern shoppers.

Insight: Information Overload, Trust Deficit

Consumers are inundated with polarized reviews and biased information. This flood of data leaves them struggling to find a credible source to guide their decisions.

“Online reviews are frustrating because they are polarizing and you can’t tell who is being honest and who is not.”

- Research Participant

Insight: Unsupported Emotional Decisions

Large purchases are emotionally charged, demanding complex trade-offs. Yet, consumers are left to navigate this journey alone, lacking the personalized guidance needed to feel confident.

Phase 2:
Defining the Core Problem

Our research pointed to a clear opportunity: evolving the "Best Time to Buy" feature from a general guide into a proactive, personalized tool that solves the "anxious waiting game."

The Waiting Game is Broken

For non-urgent purchases, consumers exist in a state of limbo. A pre-Prime Day poll showed 93% of participants were waiting to buy a specific item, hoping for a sale. The current process of tracking prices is manual, frustrating, and often forgotten.

“I continue looking and looking and looking, and I usually forget to keep looking. And then one day I remember, and it’s expensive again.”

- Research Participant

This revealed a need to help consumers not just decide, but to follow through.

Phase 3:
Designing for Trust

Automating a purchase is a big step. We knew our design had to be built on a foundation of trust, giving users full control and transparency at every step.

Control Through Optionality

In our A/B tests, users expressed discomfort with automatic purchases. In response, we made it a core principle to give users a choice: buy automatically, or get a notification to confirm. This simple choice puts the user firmly in control.

Reassurance Through Guidance

When testing a fully customizable screen versus one with presets, nearly every participant chose the preset version, calling it "faster, clearer, and more trustworthy." Our final design provides smart, pre-calculated targets to reassure users, while still allowing for full customization.

Phase 4:
Assessing Business Value

Beyond improving the member experience, our solution presents a significant opportunity to advance CR's mission and business.

Targeting Key Demographics

We predict the innovators and early adopters will be Millennials and Gen X. Millennials are "power users of AI" (24% daily usage), while Gen X accounts for over 53% of consumer spending and makes up a significant portion of CR's existing base.

Increasing Affiliate Revenue

Many "want" purchases are researched on CR, but the final click happens on a retailer's site, losing the affiliate commission. By automating these purchases, we can capture this foregone revenue.

  • ➤A 5% increase in affiliate usage could generate an additional $750,000 in revenue.
  • ➤A 10% increase could generate an additional $1.5M in annual revenue.
